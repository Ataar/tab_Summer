Ras Malai Ras Malai is a popular Indian and Bengali dessert made from soft paneer (chhena) dumplings soaked in sweet, thickened milk flavored with cardamom, saffron, and nuts. The dish has a rich, creamy texture and a delicate, melt-in-the-mouth taste. Main Ingredients: Paneer (chhena), milk, sugar, cardamom, saffron, and dry fruits like almonds and pistachios. Preparation: Small paneer balls are made by curdling milk and shaping them into soft dumplings. These are boiled in sugar syrup to expand and absorb sweetness. They are then soaked in thickened, flavored milk (rabri). Taste & Texture: Sweet, creamy, and spongy with a light saffron and cardamom aroma. Serving: Served chilled, garnished with nuts and sometimes rose water. Origin: Originally from Bengal, but now loved across India and globally.
